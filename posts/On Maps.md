This is my first poem since I was forced to write one in school several decades ago. 
# The Map is Not the Territory

When I was a child, 
I would open a book 
and be excited to see a map inside. 
I knew this was a book for me, 
it would take me to places 
that were made up. 

They were real.
There was a map.

Before I started to read, 
I looked at the names 
and wondered,
what would happen there? 
 
Smaug hovered above the Lonely Mountain, 
unmistakable.
This is a book about going to see a dragon. 
Kingdoms of the West and the Angaraks; 
Sendaria, Arendia, heroic names, 
set against grating Mishrak and Nadrak
Promises of war.

All of the maps were black on white, 
or, if the book was old or cheap enough, 
on yellow.

I still read books with maps. 
they come in colour, 
sometimes vibrant, 
neon green, rich red,
sometimes muted,
rose coloured.

Now the maps are in the back, 
I turn to the back first. 

They pull out on perforations — 
careful, 
careful they don't tear.
Shall I use a knife?
The map covers a table. 
One side inspiration, 
country, 
carnival,
one side detail, 
dark dungeons, 
or a city. 
Where will we go this time?

Perfect digital maps, 
colourful printed maps, 
old maps with creases tearing, 
black on white 
or on nostalgia yellow. 

The map is not the territory, 
but it takes you there anyway.
